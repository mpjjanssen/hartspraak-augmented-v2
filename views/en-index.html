About this therapy

What is substance-assisted psychotherapy?

Substance-assisted psychotherapy is not an alternative treatment — it is a deepening of the therapy you already know. At Hartspraak we use MDMA and psilocybine as catalysts within a carefully designed therapeutic framework, combined with somatic psychotherapy, inner child work and shadow work.

The foundation is simple but profound: trauma is not only in the mind. It lives in the body, in the muscles, in the breath, in patterns formed in earliest childhood. Conventional talk therapy often reaches the story, but not always the feeling beneath it. Substance-assisted therapy helps make those deeper layers accessible — not by surrendering control, but by gently softening defense mechanisms so your inner healing intelligence can do its work.

The substance opens the door. The therapist holds the space. But it is you who walks inside — toward yourself.
— The philosophy behind our work

Two substances, different mechanisms

MDMA and psilocybine are both powerful therapeutic tools, but they work in fundamentally different ways in the brain and in the therapeutic experience. They complement each other — and at Hartspraak they are deployed at the moment most fitting for your process.

MDMA

Mechanism: Increases serotonin, oxytocin and dopamine. Calms the amygdala (fear center) and strengthens the connection with the prefrontal cortex.

Experience: A sense of safety, openness and connection. You can process traumatic material without being overwhelmed by fear or pain.

Particularly suited for: Trauma (PTSD), attachment wounds, sexual trauma, relational patterns.

Psilocybine

Mechanism: Binds to serotonin 5-HT2A receptors. Reduces Default Mode Network activity (the "self-network") and increases neuroplasticity.

Experience: Expansion of consciousness, access to deep emotions and memories, mystical or transcendent experiences. More confronting than MDMA.

Particularly suited for: Depression, existential anxiety, addiction, rigid thought patterns, spiritual seeking.

The shared foundation

Regardless of which substance: the therapeutic relationship, preparation, setting and integration are always the foundation. The substance amplifies the process — it is not the process. At Hartspraak we work from the tradition of Reich, Lowen and Pierrakos, integrated with Jungian shadow work and attachment theory.
