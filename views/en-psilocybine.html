Science

Principles of psilocybin therapy

What is psilocybin?

Psilocybin is a naturally occurring psychedelic compound found in more than 200 species of mushrooms, often referred to as "magic mushrooms". When ingested, psilocybin is converted in the body to psilocin, which binds to serotonin receptors in the brain, particularly the 5-HT2A receptor, leading to changes in perception, mood and cognition.

Scientific research

Recent scientific research from institutions such as Johns Hopkins University and Imperial College London has shown promising results for the use of psilocybin in treating:

Depression

Treatment-resistant depression shows significant improvement after psilocybin therapy.

Anxiety disorders

Especially in life-threatening illnesses and existential distress.

Addiction

Promising results in alcoholism and nicotine addiction.

OCD

Obsessive-compulsive disorder shows improvement in early studies.

Therapeutic mechanisms

Increased neuroplasticity — Psilocybin promotes the formation of new neural connections, helping to break rigid thought patterns.

Default Mode Network modulation — Psilocybin temporarily reduces activity in the DMN, a brain network involved in self-referential thinking and rumination.

Emotional processing — The experience can provide access to suppressed emotions and memories for processing and healing.

Mystical experiences — Deep experiences of connection and unity can lead to lasting positive changes in perspective.

The importance of set and setting

Set refers to the mindset, expectations and psychological state of the person. Setting refers to the physical and social environment. An optimal therapeutic experience requires careful attention to both factors.

Preparation

Preparation guidelines

Psychological preparation

A key element for a safe psychedelic experience is your relationship with your guides. Trust in your therapist is essential — when you can trust, you are able to let go and fully immerse yourself in the experience.

Setting intentions

Reflect on what you hope to achieve: what do you want to explore or understand? What emotional or psychological issues do you want to address? Are you seeking emotional healing, creative insights or spiritual growth? A clear intention provides focus and direction, but remain open to unexpected insights.

Inner healing intelligence

At the heart of psychedelic therapy lies the concept of inner healing wisdom. There is in each individual a deep aspect of the psyche that is naturally driven toward healing and integration. During the experience, defense mechanisms are lifted and the inner healing intelligence takes control.

Mental preparation

Understand the process — Familiarize yourself with the role of psilocybin and what to expect during and after the session.

Practice relaxation techniques — Deep breathing, meditation or gentle yoga in the days before your session.

Cultivate a positive mindset — Focus on the potential for growth and healing. Challenging moments can lead to profound insights.

Communicate with your therapist — Open communication about concerns, intentions and expectations is essential.

Physical preparation

1

Healthy lifestyle

Balanced diet, regular exercise and adequate sleep in the weeks prior.

2

Avoid substances

No alcohol or recreational drugs at least 48 hours before the session.

3

Comfort

Wear comfortable clothing and bring personal items that provide comfort.

Emotional preparation

Embrace vulnerability — understand that you may be confronted with challenging emotions. Identify sources of pain and discover internal stories. No one can predict how the journey will unfold; preparation involves developing readiness and openness for whatever experience arises.

Nutrition

Nutrition recommendations and supplements

General nutrition guidelines

Weeks before the session

Follow a balanced diet: plenty of fresh vegetables and fruits, beans and legumes, whole grains, nuts and seeds. Limit processed foods and foods high in salt, sugar and animal fats.

48 hours before the session

Abstain from alcohol and recreational drugs. Avoid caffeine on the day of the session. Eat light, easily digestible meals in the 24 hours prior.

Day of the session

Eat a light, nutritious meal 2-3 hours before the session. Ensure good hydration but avoid excessive drinking just before the session.

Foods to avoid

Limit tyramine-rich foods

Aged cheeses, fermented sausages, smoked meats, soy sauce, tempeh, sauerkraut, kimchi, overripe avocados, dried fruits, draft beer and certain wines. Also avoid heavy fatty meals and strongly spiced foods.

Supplements

Potentially supportive

Magnesium — helps with muscle relaxation. B vitamins — support the nervous system. Omega-3 fatty acids — support brain health. Adaptogenic herbs — ashwagandha or rhodiola for stress reduction.

To avoid

5-HTP and tryptophan — risk of serotonin syndrome. St. John's Wort — affects serotonin levels. Stimulating supplements — caffeine and guarana can worsen anxiety.

Medication considerations

Always consult with your therapist and doctor about medications, especially SSRIs/SNRIs, MAO inhibitors and benzodiazepines. Never change your medication without consulting your doctor.

The experience

Expectations during the session

Phases of the session

1

Arrival and preparation

30-60 minutes. The therapist shows the space, explains the process and answers final questions. Often includes a short meditation or breathing exercise.

2

Ingestion and beginning

30-60 minutes. After ingestion, effects begin. You lie quietly with an eye mask and headphones with music.

3

Peak experience

2-3 hours. The most intense effects, 60-90 minutes after ingestion. This is where the most significant breakthroughs occur.

Total duration

The full session typically lasts 6-8 hours. It is important to keep the entire day free, with no obligations after the session.

Physical experiences

Altered body sensation

Lightness or heaviness, or the sensation that your body is larger or smaller.

Tingling and energy

Warmth or flows of energy through the body.

Nausea

Mild nausea is possible, especially at the beginning. Usually subsides after 30-60 minutes.

Heightened sensitivity

Senses are enhanced — touches, sounds and temperatures feel more intense.

Sensory experiences

With eyes closed, you may see vivid images, patterns, colors and complete scenes. With eyes open, surfaces may seem to breathe or wave. Music sounds deeper and richer. Some experience synesthesia — "seeing" music as colors.

Emotional and psychological experiences

Heightened emotional openness — Greater access to emotions, both positive and challenging.

Emotional processing — Suppressed emotions surface for processing and healing.

Ego dissolution — Boundaries of the self may blur, which can lead to a sense of unity.

Altered sense of time — Moments may seem endless or pass very quickly.

The therapist remains present throughout the session as a safe anchor and guide, with a non-directive approach that trusts the inner healing intelligence.
— The role of the therapist during the session

Music and the experience

Music plays a central role: carefully selected music guides the emotional journey, provides structure to the experience, and can serve as a vehicle for emotional processing.

Navigating challenges

Surrender, breathing and support

Learn to surrender to the experience rather than trying to control it. Returning to breathing helps ground you. The therapist is trained to navigate you through challenging moments.

After the session

Integration practices

What is integration?

Integration is the process by which experiences, insights and emotions from the session are processed and incorporated into daily life: making meaning, processing emotions, translating insights into concrete changes, and internalizing the experience.

Integration timeline

24-72 hours

Immediate aftermath

Rest and reflection. Begin documenting your experience. Plan minimal obligations.

1-2 weeks

Short term

Integration sessions with your therapist. Begin implementing daily practices. Consider integration groups.

Weeks to months

Long term

Ongoing reflection. Sustainable lifestyle adjustments. Periodic check-ins with your therapist.

Integration techniques

Journaling

Free writing, guided prompts, visual journaling. Combine words with drawings and symbols.

Meditation and mindfulness

Guided meditation on insights, body scans, breathwork for emotional processing.

Creative expression

Drawing, painting, music, dance and movement to express non-verbal aspects.

Somatic integration

Yoga, tai chi, qigong and nature walks support the physical integration process.

Community

Integration groups, one-on-one support, and sharing with trusted people.

Lifestyle adjustments

Nutrition, sleep, exercise and time in nature. Small, achievable steps rather than all at once.

Patience and compassion

Integration is an ongoing process that requires time, dedication and support. There is no "right" way — the process is personal and unique. Be patient and compassionate with yourself, and seek professional support if needed.

Psilocybin truffles: types, dosages and effects

What are psilocybin truffles?

Psilocybin truffles (sclerotia) are compact masses of hardened mycelium — the underground "roots" of mushrooms — that contain psilocybin and psilocin. They form as a survival mechanism of the fungus under unfavorable conditions.

Microdosing

Sub-perceptual dose

Microdosing is taking a dose that does not lead to noticeable psychedelic effects. Fresh truffles: 0.5-1 gram. Dried truffles: 0.1-0.3 gram. Used by some for enhanced creativity, focus and mood.

Timeline of effects

30-60 min

Onset

First effects become noticeable.

60-90 min

Come up

Effects intensify.

2-3 hours

Peak

Maximum intensity of the experience.

1-2 hours

Plateau and decline

Gradual return to normal consciousness.

Total duration: usually 4-6 hours, depending on dosage and individual factors.

Safety considerations

For beginners

Always start with a low dose. Ensure a safe environment. Have a sober, experienced trip sitter. Do not combine with other drugs or alcohol.

For experienced users

Account for tolerance. Allow sufficient integration time. Increase doses gradually. Be aware of potency differences between species.
