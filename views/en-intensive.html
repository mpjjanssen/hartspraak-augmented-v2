The Intensive

Five days, deep transformation

A carefully structured journey of preparation, experience and integration. Not a quick fix, but a path to the core of what holds you — and what can set you free.

The program Preparation

Our approach

The philosophy behind the intensive

At Hartstraak, the intensive is not simply a "session with a substance." It is a carefully orchestrated therapeutic process rooted in decades of experience with body-oriented psychotherapy in the tradition of Reich, Lowen and Pierrakos, combined with Jungian shadow work, inner child work and attachment theory.

The substance — whether MDMA or psilocybin — is a catalyst. It strengthens and deepens the therapeutic work already underway. True transformation takes place in the relationship: with yourself, with your body, with your pain, and with the therapist who guides you.

The body as entry point — Trauma and early wounds are stored in the body, in muscle patterns, in your breath, in how you hold yourself. Body-oriented therapy makes these patterns conscious and accessible.

Character structures — We work with six character structures (Avoider, Seeker, Merger, Bearer, Strategist, Achiever) that show how you learned to survive as a child — and how those strategies still hold you.

The inner child — Behind every character structure is a child who was not seen, not heard, or not protected. The intensive provides space to meet and heal that child.

The shadow — In the Jungian tradition, the shadow is what we do not want to see. The substance helps you meet the shadow with compassion instead of fear.

The program

Five days — day by day

The intensive takes place in a quiet, safe environment. Five days in which you can fully land, let go and transform. Each day has its own function in the larger whole. You arrive on day 1 around 4:00 PM and depart on day 5 around 12:00 PM.

Day 1 — Arrival and landing (from 4:00 PM)

Grounding and connection

Arrival, orientation and creating a safe foundation. Body-oriented exercises to land in your body. Getting to know the space and the facilitators. Discussing intentions and expectations. The foundation for trust.

Day 2 — Deepening

The body speaks

Intensive body work to bring conscious awareness to the themes at play. Breathwork, movement and expression. Exploring your character structure and the protective patterns you have developed.

Day 3 — Further deepening

The mirror work

Further deepening via character structure using Mirror Work. Exploring your unconscious patterns, your defense strategies and the discrepancy between who you think you are and how you act unconsciously. An essential preparation for tomorrow's session.

Day 4 — The session

The door opens

The guided session with MDMA or psilocybin. In a warm, safe environment, with music and the constant presence of your therapist. This is the moment when defense mechanisms soften and your inner healing intelligence can do its work. The duration varies from 6 to 8 hours.

Day 5 — Integration and departure (until ±12:00 PM)

Landing, processing and moving forward

The morning after the session is crucial. Rest, reflection and initial processing of the experience. Creative expression — drawing, writing, music — to give form to what cannot be put into words. Translating the experience into concrete steps in your daily life. What do you take with you? What do you leave behind? Creating an integration plan for the weeks and months ahead. Departure with solid ground beneath your feet.

MDMA or psilocybin?

The choice of substance is made together with your therapist, based on your therapeutic themes, your history and your personal process. MDMA offers more warmth and safety; psilocybin offers more depth and confrontation. Sometimes a different substance is used in a later intensive.

Session transcription

If you wish, all sessions during the intensive can be transcribed. This gives you the opportunity to revisit the process afterwards — words, insights and breakthroughs that are sometimes difficult to hold in the moment. The transcriptions become part of your personal file and can be valuable for the integration process.

Before the intensive

Preparation

Preparation for an intensive begins weeks before you arrive. It is an essential part of the process — the better prepared you are, the deeper the work can go.

1

Intake and screening

Comprehensive intake interview, medical screening and mapping your therapeutic themes and goals.

2

Online preparation

Via the online preparation portal, you work on specific assignments, reflection questions and body-oriented exercises.

3

Body and lifestyle

Guidelines for nutrition, movement, sleep and tapering off certain substances in the weeks before the intensive.

The preparation portal

After registration, you will receive access to intensive.hartspraak.com, where you will be guided step by step through the preparation. You will also find your personal file and the Mirror Work test here.

After the intensive

Integration and aftercare

The intensive is the beginning, not the end. True change takes place in the weeks and months afterwards, when you integrate the insights into your daily life.

Integration sessions

Scheduled sessions with your therapist to process the experience and translate it into concrete changes.

The year-long program

Many participants continue with the Bronwerk (year 1) or Wonderwerk (year 2) year-long program for deeper integration.

Creative processing

Writing, drawing, music — creative expression is a powerful tool to integrate the experience.

Community

Connection with others going through the same process. You are not alone on this journey.
