The research

What the science says

Groundbreaking research from Johns Hopkins University — published in March 2026 in JAMA Network Open — has shown that psilocybin combined with cognitive behavioral therapy is significantly more effective than nicotine patches for quitting smoking.

Participants in the study

Participants smoked an average of 16 cigarettes per day and had previously made an average of six failed quit attempts. After a single dose of psilocybin, combined with a 13-week cognitive behavioral therapy program, 40.5% remained smoke-free after six months — compared to only 10% in the nicotine patch group.

Quitting smoking is not a simple biological reaction to psilocybin. But when it is administered after careful preparation and in a therapeutic context, it can lead to deep reflection about your life and motivation to change.
— Dr. Matthew Johnson, Johns Hopkins University

Mechanism of action

Why psilocybin works differently

The fundamental difference from conventional methods: nicotine patches, gum, and medications like varenicline all work on nicotine receptors — they replace or block nicotine's effects. Psilocybin works on an entirely different level.

Conventional approach

Focuses on chemical addiction: replaces nicotine or blocks receptors. Doesn't address the underlying patterns, habits, and psychological dependence.

PsiloReset approach

Focuses on deeper patterns: breaks rigid thinking patterns, increases neuroplasticity, and creates a shift in perspective that fundamentally changes your relationship with smoking.

Three mechanisms of action

Neuroplasticity — Psilocybin promotes the formation of new neural connections, allowing the rigid patterns that sustain addiction to be broken.

Perspective shift — Participants describe a fundamental change in how they view smoking — no longer as a need, but as something that doesn't belong to them.

Internal motivation — Rather than willpower or replacement, an intrinsic motivation to quit emerges, often linked to deeper insights about life quality and values.

It's not the substance alone

Researchers emphasize: it's the combination of psilocybin with intensive therapeutic guidance that makes the difference. The neuroplastic and learning effects that occur after exposure are leveraged within a structured therapeutic framework.

The journey

What PsiloReset looks like

The PsiloReset journey at Hartspraak combines scientific insights from Johns Hopkins research with our body-centered therapeutic approach. It is an intensive program that goes beyond just quitting smoking — it explores why you smoke.

Phase 1

Preparation and awareness

Therapeutic sessions to explore your smoking patterns: when, why, what emotions or situations trigger smoking? Body-centered work to become aware of the tension you "manage" with smoking. Setting intentions.

Phase 2

The psilocybin session

A guided psilocybin experience on or around your planned quit date. In a safe, warm environment with music and professional support. Aimed at breaking the patterns that keep you smoking.

Phase 3

Integration and aftercare

Processing the experience. Developing new strategies for moments of craving. Body-centered exercises to regulate tension in other ways. Long-term support.
